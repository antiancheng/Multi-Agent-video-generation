T1：The child holding a carp spins in a circle while holding the fish.

[Main Prompt]

Preparation: The Bao Yu Wa Wa (child holding a carp) tightly clutches a large fish with golden scales and a red tail. Standing slightly on tiptoe to gather momentum, the waist twists gently; the folds of the clothing and bellyband (Dudou) rise and fall slightly with breathing. The gaze is focused downward, as if concentrating on initiating a spinning motion.

Climax: The body suddenly spins up, arms steadily holding the fish as if embracing a treasure. The fish tail flings outward with a luminous arc due to centrifugal force. Brocade sleeves and ribbons fly with the momentum; red fluff on the hair bun trembles. The embroidered shoes trace a circular path on the ground. The entire movement is coordinated, as if a New Year print has come to life.

Conclusion: The spinning momentum gradually slows; the child lands lightly in place, knees slightly bent to absorb the force. The goldfish in the arms still glimmers, a smile lingers on the lips, and the eyes ripple with residual charm, as if the auspicious and festive atmosphere continues to spread endlessly with the rotation.

Style: Distinct Yangliuqing style—large head and rounded body, pink face and vermilion lips. Dressed in a red-green contrasting bellyband and blue-bordered brocade trousers. The goldfish scales are traced with gold powder. The overall coloring is rich yet elegant, with smooth and full lines, filled with folk festive charm.
