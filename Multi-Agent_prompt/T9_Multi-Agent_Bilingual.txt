T9:The child holding a carp is sulking.

[Main Prompt]

Preparation: The Bao Yu Wa Wa (child holding a carp) tightly clutches the goldfish in the arms. The mouth is slightly pouted, and the brows are lightly furrowed. The gaze is cast sideways toward the ground. The chest rises and falls slightly, causing the fine wrinkles on the red bellyband (Dudou) and blue cloth trousers to tremble gently. The toes unconsciously rub against the blue brick floor.

Climax: The child suddenly presses the goldfish harder into the chest; the chubby body tenses up accordingly. The "lotus-root-like" (segmented and chubby) arm muscles bulge slightly. The goldfish’s tail fin splashes water droplets due to the squeeze. The lapel and hair braids fling out in arcs with the movement; the cheeks are puffed out as if holding walnuts.

Conclusion: As the force gradually fades, the child’s shoulders slowly slump. The chin rests against the fish’s back. Eyelids are lowered but still reveal a sense of stubbornness. The goldfish gently sways its tail in the crook of the arm, rippling out an imperceptible circle. Residual anger remains hidden in the lingering curve of the mouth.

Style: Typical Yangliuqing woodblock print coloration—vermilion bellyband, indigo trousers, and peach-pink cheeks with ink-line outlines. The clothing patterns are intricate but organized. Goldfish scales are dotted with gold leaf. The overall silhouette is rounded and full, revealing the naive and simple rhythm of folk woodblock prints within the balance of motion and stillness.
