T3:The child holding a carp slips on a lotus leaf.

[Main Prompt]

Preparation: The Bao Yu Wa Wa (child holding a carp) stands at the center of a rounded lotus leaf on slight tiptoe. The arms embracing the golden-scaled koi tighten slightly. The sleeves tremble as the center of gravity shifts forward. The gaze is focused on the water ripples ahead, while the red bellyband (Dudou) and emerald green trouser legs rise and fall subtly with breathing.

Climax: The lotus leaf suddenly slips. The child’s waist twists as balance is lost, leaning backward with legs splayed and kicking the air. The goldfish in the arms thrashes its tail, splashing water droplets. Satin sleeves and ribbons unfurl like rolling ripples; the fluff balls on the hair bun shake violently. The entire body slides rapidly along the curvature of the leaf surface.

Conclusion: The child falls and sits at the heart of the leaf, which bounces and trembles slightly under the impact. One hand still tightly clutches the fish’s belly while the other braces against the leaf to steady the remaining momentum. The cheeks are flushed, and the eyes are wide with a smile. Water droplets slide slowly from the edge of the clothes, and the ripples on the water have not yet subsided.

Style: Typical Yangliuqing woodblock print color scheme—interweaving vermilion, indigo, and emerald green. The child’s face is plump and fair. The clothing is adorned with auspicious cloud patterns and hidden embroidery of fish scales. The overall silhouette is rounded, naive, and festive, maintaining the flat decorative rhythm of traditional woodblock prints within the dynamic motion.
