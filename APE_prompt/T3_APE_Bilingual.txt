T3：抱鱼娃娃在荷叶上滑倒了

（主体）一个典型的杨柳青年画风格抱鱼娃娃，头扎双髻，面如满月，白里透红，眉眼弯弯带笑，采用传统“开脸”技法勾勒出憨态可掬的神情；身着大红肚兜，上绣金线鲤鱼纹样，脚蹬虎头鞋，怀中原本紧抱一条肥硕红鳞鲤鱼。
（动作）娃娃站在一片硕大碧绿的荷叶上，因叶面湿滑突然失衡踉跄，身体后仰，双臂慌乱挥舞，怀中鲤鱼趁机扭身挣脱，鱼尾高高扬起溅起晶莹水珠；娃娃表情由得意转为惊愕，却仍带着孩童特有的天真笑意，整个人即将跌入水中却又未完全倒下，定格在动态张力最强的一瞬。
（风格）整体画面延续杨柳青木版年画的非遗美学：线条遒劲流畅，色彩厚重如矿物研磨而成——朱砂红、石青绿、泥金黄层层套印；背景保留手工水印的微妙错版痕迹与木纹肌理，营造出既喜庆又古朴的民间艺术氛围。
（环境）场景设于夏日荷塘中央，荷叶层叠如盖，粉白荷花半开，几片花瓣随风飘落水面；远处隐约可见芦苇摇曳，水面泛起涟漪，倒映天光云影；整个空间仿佛铺展于一张泛黄宣纸上，边缘略带岁月晕染的斑驳感。
（镜头）采用电影级慢速环绕镜头，从娃娃正面特写缓缓拉远至全景，捕捉其滑倒瞬间的肢体动态与水花飞溅细节；高帧率摄影强化动作流畅性，浅景深虚化背景突出主体，柔光自斜上方洒落，在荷叶露珠与鱼鳞上折射出温润光泽，赋予传统年画以鲜活的生命律动。

T3：The child holding a carp slips on a lotus leaf.

(Subject): A typical Bao Yu Wa Wa (child holding a fish) in the Yangliuqing woodblock print style, featuring twin hair buns and a face as round as a full moon with a rosy glow. The child has curving eyebrows and a smiling expression, with a naive and charming countenance outlined using the traditional Kailian (facial painting) technique. Dressed in a bright red bellyband (Dudou) embroidered with golden-threaded carp patterns and wearing tiger-head shoes, the child was originally clutching a plump, red-scaled carp tightly in their arms.

(Action): Standing on a massive, emerald-green lotus leaf, the child suddenly loses balance and stumbles due to the slippery surface. As the body leans backward and arms wave frantically, the carp seizes the opportunity to twist and break free, its tail raised high and splashing crystal water droplets. The child's expression shifts from smugness to astonishment, yet still carries a characteristic innocent smile. The entire figure is poised on the verge of falling into the water but hasn't completely collapsed, frozen at the moment of peak dynamic tension.

(Style): The overall visual continues the Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH) aesthetics of Yangliuqing woodblock prints: lines are vigorous and smooth; colors are rich and heavy as if ground from minerals—featuring layered overprinting of cinnabar red, mineral green, and liquid gold. The background retains subtle registration misalignment marks and wood grain textures from manual water-printing, creating a folk art atmosphere that is both festive and primitive.

(Environment): The scene is set in the center of a summer lotus pond, with lotus leaves layered like a canopy and pinkish-white lotuses half-bloomed. A few petals drift onto the water with the wind. Reeds are faintly visible swaying in the distance, while ripples spread across the water, reflecting the sky and clouds. The entire space appears as if spread across a sheet of yellowed Xuan paper, with edges showing the mottled effect of aged ink bleeding.

(Camera): Utilizing a cinematic slow orbiting shot, the camera slowly pulls back from a close-up of the child's face to a panoramic view, capturing the body dynamics of the slip and the details of splashing water. High frame rate photography enhances the smoothness of the motion, while a shallow depth of field blurs the background to highlight the subject. Soft light pours down from a diagonal angle, refracting a warm glow on the lotus leaf dewdrops and fish scales, imbuing the traditional New Year print with a vivid, rhythmic vitality.
