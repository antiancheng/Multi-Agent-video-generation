T7：抱鱼娃娃被吓了一跳

（主体）一个圆润可爱的杨柳青年画风格抱鱼娃娃，头扎双髻，面如满月，身穿红绿相间的传统肚兜与裤子，怀中紧抱一条鳞光闪闪的大鲤鱼，鱼尾微翘，鱼眼圆睁。
（动作）突然间，娃娃瞪大双眼，嘴巴微张成“O”形，脸颊鼓起，身体猛地后仰，双臂本能收紧将鲤鱼搂得更紧；鲤鱼受惊甩尾，水珠四溅，娃娃脚下一滑，一只绣花小鞋差点脱落，发髻上的红绒球随之剧烈晃动。
（风格）延续清代鼎盛期杨柳青年画的细腻工笔与木版套色技法：面部采用“开脸”传统，突出“三白”（额头、鼻梁、下颌）敷粉；衣纹用墨线勾勒后填染矿物颜料，红如朱砂、绿似石青；背景留白处理，强调主体动态；整体保留木版套印错位感微弱的古拙韵味，兼具喜庆与童趣。
（环境）无具体场景，仅以淡米黄宣纸肌理为底，隐约可见木版水印的纤维纹理，营造年画张贴于老宅门板或炕围的民俗氛围。
（镜头）特写近景，略带仰角捕捉娃娃受惊瞬间的夸张表情与肢体张力；焦点清晰落在娃娃圆睁的双眼与鲤鱼闪亮的鳞片上，背景虚化但保留纸本质感；画面右上角可加一枚朱文闲章“吉庆有余”，强化非遗年画语境。

T7：The child holding a carp gets startled.

(Subject): A plump and cute Bao Yu Wa Wa (child holding a fish) in the Yangliuqing woodblock print style. Features twin hair buns and a face as round as a full moon. Dressed in a traditional red and green contrasting bellyband (Dudou) and trousers. The child tightly embraces a large carp with shimmering scales; the fish's tail is slightly upturned, and its eyes are wide open.

(Action): Suddenly, the child's eyes widen, the mouth parts slightly into an "O" shape, and the cheeks puff out. The body recoils sharply, and the arms instinctively tighten to hold the carp even closer. Startled, the carp thrashes its tail, splashing water droplets. The child's foot slips, with one embroidered shoe nearly coming off, while the red fluff balls on the hair bun shake violently.

(Style): Continuing the delicate fine-line brushwork (Gongbi) and woodblock polychrome printing techniques from the peak of the Qing Dynasty's Yangliuqing prints. The face follows the "Kailian" tradition, highlighting the Sanbai (Three Whites) technique (applying white powder to the forehead, bridge of the nose, and chin). Clothing folds are outlined with ink lines and filled with mineral pigments—red like cinnabar and green like mineral blue. The background utilizes negative space to emphasize the subject's movement. The overall aesthetic retains the primitive charm of subtle woodblock registration misalignments, blending festivity with childlike playfulness.

(Environment): No specific scene; utilizing a pale beige Xuan paper texture as the base. The fibrous texture of the woodblock watermark is faintly visible, evoking the folk atmosphere of a New Year print pasted on an old house door or a bed enclosure (Kangwei).

(Camera): A close-up shot from a slight low angle, capturing the exaggerated expression and physical tension at the moment the child is startled. The focus rests sharply on the child's wide eyes and the shimmering scales of the carp. The background is blurred while preserving the paper texture. An auspicious red seal "Ji Qing You Yu" (Auspiciousness and Abundance) is placed in the upper right corner to reinforce the Intangible Cultural Heritage context.
